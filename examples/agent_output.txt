The patient is a 56-year-old female who presented to the emergency department following a fall. She fell onto cement after missing two steps while searching for a physical therapy office. The patient landed directly on her left leg and developed pain and swelling above the left knee. There is a large bruise measuring 8x8 cm with an overlying 5 cm laceration. The left ankle is also swollen but not painful. The patient was able to walk immediately after the fall but the pain worsened while shopping and driving. She takes blood pressure medication and occasionally uses aspirin. Her last tetanus shot was 6 years ago. Physical examination shows significant swelling and bruising on the inside of the left knee with a laceration present.
